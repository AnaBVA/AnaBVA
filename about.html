About







  • Name: Ana Beatriz Villaseñor-Altamirano

  • Bachelor: Biochemical Engineering

Before working in bioinformatics, I did wet lab expirience working with spermatogenesis in high-school, and later in hepatocarcinogenesis for my undergrad thesis in Saúl Villa-Treviño’s lab. I characterized the protein expression of Insulin-Like Growth Factor Binding Protein II (IGFBP2) during the develompent of cancer in rat livers using blood and tissue.

I graduated from the Undergraduate Program on Biochemical Engineering at National Biological Sience School (ENCB), IPN in 2012.

After that, I attended and graduated from the Master of Science in cell biology programm at CINVESTAV in Guillermo Elizondo-Azuela’s lab in collaboration with Paul Boutros. My master’s thesis aim was to unscramble the alternative splicing effects of TCDD in C57BL6 mouse hepatocytes using high-throughput data.
